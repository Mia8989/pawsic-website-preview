# Skin Failure Shared Interest Group -- Forum Rules & Community Guidelines

**Premium SIG Space | PAWSIC Community**

---

Welcome to the **Skin Failure Shared Interest Group (SIG)** -- a premium PAWSIC community space dedicated to advancing the understanding, assessment, and documentation of skin failure in post-acute and end-of-life care settings.

This forum exists to facilitate evidence-based dialogue among wound care professionals. The following rules ensure our discussions remain clinically rigorous, ethically sound, and beneficial for all members. By participating, you agree to these guidelines.

---

## 1. Purpose & Scope

The Skin Failure SIG focuses on:

- **Skin failure recognition and assessment** -- including distinguishing skin failure from pressure injuries, moisture-associated skin damage (MASD), and other etiologies
- **End-of-life skin changes** -- Kennedy Terminal Ulcers (KTU), Skin Changes at Life's End (SCALE), and Trombley-Brennan Terminal Tissue Injuries (TB-TTI)
- **Documentation and regulatory considerations** -- CMS survey readiness, MDS coding, care planning, and family communication
- **Emerging research and clinical evidence** -- peer-reviewed findings, clinical frameworks, and evolving definitions
- **Interdisciplinary perspectives** -- nursing, palliative care, hospice, long-term care, dermatology, and wound care specialists

Discussions outside this scope may be redirected to the appropriate PAWSIC community forum or SIG space.

---

## 2. Who Can Participate

The Skin Failure SIG is available exclusively to **PAWSIC Premium Members**. Participants typically include:

- Wound, Ostomy, and Continence (WOC) nurses and certified wound care specialists
- Directors of Nursing (DONs) and clinical leadership in post-acute settings
- Palliative care and hospice clinicians
- Physicians, nurse practitioners, and physician assistants specializing in wound care
- Researchers and academics studying skin failure and end-of-life skin changes
- Quality improvement and regulatory compliance professionals

> **Credential Transparency:** We encourage all members to list their credentials and practice setting in their profile. This helps the community contextualize clinical perspectives and fosters trust in discussions.

## 3. Core Forum Rules

### 3.1 Evidence-Based Discussion

- All clinical claims, treatment recommendations, and assessment guidance **must** be supported by peer-reviewed evidence, clinical practice guidelines, or recognized expert consensus
- When citing evidence, include the source (author, journal/publication, year) whenever possible
- Acceptable sources include peer-reviewed journals, Cochrane Reviews, NPUAP/EPUAP/PPPIA guidelines, WOCN position statements, and AHRQ publications
- Clearly distinguish between established evidence, emerging research, clinical opinion, and anecdotal experience
- Statements like "in my clinical experience" are valued but should be identified as experiential rather than evidence-based

### 3.2 Patient Privacy (HIPAA Compliance)

> **CRITICAL -- ZERO TOLERANCE:** HIPAA compliance is mandatory and non-negotiable. Violations will result in immediate post removal and may result in permanent forum ban. PAWSIC is obligated to report suspected HIPAA violations to the appropriate authorities.

- **Never** share patient names, initials, dates of birth, facility names, or any combination of details that could identify a patient
- **Never** post clinical photographs that include identifiable patient features (face, tattoos, birthmarks, room numbers, facility branding) without documented HIPAA authorization
- When discussing clinical scenarios, use de-identified composite cases: "An 82-year-old female in a skilled nursing facility presented with..."
- Remove all 18 HIPAA identifiers per the Safe Harbor de-identification standard
- If you are uncertain whether information is identifiable, err on the side of caution and do not post it

### 3.3 Professional Conduct

- Treat all members with professional respect regardless of credentials, experience level, or practice setting
- Disagree constructively -- challenge ideas with evidence, not personal attacks
- No harassment, discrimination, bullying, or intimidation of any kind
- Avoid condescending language toward members from different disciplines or care settings
- Respect that skin failure is a complex and evolving area where clinical opinions may differ -- approach disagreements as learning opportunities

### 3.4 No Product Promotion or Commercial Bias

- Do not promote specific commercial products, devices, or services
- When discussing product categories (e.g., wound dressings, skin protectants), use generic or category names
- If a brand name is necessary for clinical context, include relevant alternatives for balanced representation
- Do not post affiliate links, referral codes, or promotional materials
- If you have a financial relationship with a company whose products are relevant to a discussion, disclose it

> **Acceptable example:** "We've had success using silicone-bordered foam dressings for sacral skin protection in hospice patients" is appropriate. "Buy [Brand X] -- it's the best!" is not.

### 3.5 No Direct Clinical Advice

- This forum is for **peer education and professional discussion**, not for providing direct clinical recommendations for specific patients
- Do not ask the forum to diagnose a specific wound or prescribe treatment for an active patient case
- When discussing clinical approaches, frame responses as general educational guidance: "In similar presentations, the literature supports..." rather than "You should do X for this patient"
- If a member is seeking urgent clinical guidance, redirect them to the appropriate clinical consult process within their facility

### 3.6 Respect Intellectual Property

- Do not post copyrighted materials (full journal articles, textbook chapters, proprietary assessment tools) without permission
- Sharing brief citations, abstracts, or links to published work is encouraged
- If sharing presentation slides or original educational materials, ensure you have the right to distribute them
- Always credit the original author or creator when referencing someone else's work

## 4. Skin Failure -- Specific Discussion Guidelines

Given the specialized nature of this SIG, the following additional guidelines apply:

### 4.1 Terminology

- Use precise, current terminology consistent with published literature and consensus documents
- Recognize that "skin failure" terminology is still evolving -- be open to discussing various frameworks and definitions
- Common accepted terms: skin failure, Kennedy Terminal Ulcer (KTU), Skin Changes at Life's End (SCALE), Trombley-Brennan Terminal Tissue Injury (TB-TTI), unavoidable pressure injury
- When introducing less-established terminology, provide context and cite the source

### 4.2 Clinical Photography

- Clinical photographs may be shared for educational purposes **only** if fully de-identified and HIPAA-compliant
- Photos must have no patient identifiers visible (face, name bands, facility signage, room numbers, unique markings)
- Include relevant clinical context: wound location, measurements, patient age range, care setting, and relevant comorbidities
- Label photographs as "used with HIPAA authorization" or "de-identified composite" as applicable
- PAWSIC moderators reserve the right to remove any photo that may compromise patient privacy

### 4.3 End-of-Life Sensitivity

- Discussions about end-of-life skin changes involve deeply sensitive clinical and human situations -- maintain compassion and sensitivity at all times
- Focus discussions on clinical best practices, documentation approaches, and family/caregiver education strategies
- Respect that clinicians may have differing perspectives on palliative vs. curative goals of care as they relate to skin integrity
- When discussing family communication about skin failure, emphasize empathy and shared decision-making

### 4.4 Regulatory & Documentation Discussions

- Discussions about CMS survey processes, MDS coding, and regulatory compliance are encouraged but must reflect current published CMS guidance
- Do not present personal interpretations of survey or coding rules as definitive guidance
- Clearly distinguish between CMS requirements, state-specific regulations, and organizational policies
- Share documentation templates or frameworks as "examples" rather than prescriptive standards

## 5. Posting Best Practices

- **Use descriptive thread titles** -- "Differentiating skin failure from deep tissue pressure injury in hospice patients" is more helpful than "Quick question"
- **Search before posting** -- Check if your topic has been discussed recently to avoid duplicates
- **Stay on topic** -- Keep replies relevant to the thread. Start a new thread for a new topic
- **Format for readability** -- Use paragraphs, bullet points, and headers when making longer posts
- **Include context** -- When asking a question, provide relevant clinical context (care setting, patient population, specific challenge) while maintaining de-identification
- **Acknowledge uncertainty** -- It is perfectly acceptable (and encouraged) to say "I don't know" or "I'm still learning about this area"
- **Close the loop** -- If you asked a question and found the answer, share it with the community
