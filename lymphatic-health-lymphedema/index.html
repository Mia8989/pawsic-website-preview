Lymphatic Health: Recognizing & Treating Lymphedema

Foundational education on lymphatic insufficiency and lymphedema for post-acute care clinicians. Learn why early recognition matters, how comprehensive care supports improved outcomes, and how integrated lymphedema management strengthens your facility's wound and skin integrity program.

Learning Objectives

What You'll Learn

Practical education for post-acute clinical teams, designed to translate lymphatic health concepts into everyday recognition, intervention, and program-level care decisions.

01
Objective 01

Recognition & Awareness

Understand lymphatic insufficiency, identify clinical signs of lymphedema, and explain why early recognition matters in post-acute care settings.

02
Objective 02

Early Intervention & Outcomes

Describe how timely recognition and treatment of lymphedema may support improved patient outcomes, comfort, and overall satisfaction with care.

03
Objective 03

Comprehensive Care Integration

Examine the operational and patient-care impact of integrating lymphedema management into a facility's wound and skin integrity program.

Faculty

Your Instructor

This course is presented by a nationally recognized lymphatic and compression specialist with advanced clinical and research credentials in edema and wound care.

Suzie Ehmann

PT, DPT, PhD, CWS, CLWT, CLT-LANA

Suzie Ehmann is a Clinical Specialist with Haddenham Healthcare, recently transitioned from McLeod Seacoast where she led the Edema Management Program. For over 20 years she has focused on the evaluation and treatment of lymphatic disorders and chronic wounds, with a special interest in compression science and its impact on functional ability and quality of life. She earned her PhD studying the biophysical effects of an alternating compression profile and contributed to the development of the STRIDE Compression Selection Algorithm. A Certified Lymphedema Therapist through the Lymphology Association of North America and a Certified Wound Specialist through the American Board of Wound Management, she has presented at prominent national and international lymphatic and wound conferences and authored multiple peer-reviewed articles.
