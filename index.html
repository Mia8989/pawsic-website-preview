Who We Serve

Who We Serve

PAWSIC brings together the full post-acute skin and wound team. If you touch skin or wounds outside the emergency, inpatient, or surgical setting, PAWSIC is your home.

Caregivers & CNAs

Frontline caregivers and certified nursing assistants providing hands-on wound and skin care.

Nurses & Wound Clinicians

RNs, LPNs, and wound care specialists across all post-acute settings.

Physicians, NPs, PAs & DPMs

Medical directors, physicians, nurse practitioners, physician assistants, and podiatrists.

Therapists (PT, OT, SLP)

Physical, occupational, and speech-language therapists involved in wound and skin care.

Post-Acute Leaders & Operators

SNF, long-term care, home health, hospice, and rehabilitation leadership.

Pharmacists, Dietitians & Specialists

Allied health professionals supporting integumentary care in post-acute settings.

Industry, Researchers & Students

Industry reps, clinical researchers, and students advancing wound care science.

Patients & Their Families

Patients navigating wound and skin care, and the families who support them.

Why PAWSIC

Skin and wound care in post-acute settings is fragmented.

Clinicians and caregivers work in silos, and patients fall through the cracks when care transitions. PAWSIC was created to:

Unite members in the post-acute care community around integumentary health
Provide evidence-based, practical education tailored to real-world post-acute care settings
Advocate for better standards, language, and continuity for skin and wound care

We are a 501(c)(6) nonprofit council led by nationally recognized post-acute wound experts.

Our Mission

We are a patient-centered inter-professional team that serves the post-acute care community as the source and voice to guide in high-quality wound healing, wound prevention, and the prevention of skin injury and breakdown. We do this through collaborative evidence-based education, practice, and advocacy.

Watch Video

Our Vision

A new paradigm for wound management that shifts away from the institutional-centric wound-focused approach and towards a patient-centric approach. A new post-acute wound management framework that leverages evidence-based practices and best expertise to address the complexity of the wounded patient.
