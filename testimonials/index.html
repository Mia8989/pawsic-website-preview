Featured Member Spotlight

Alec McCall & William Ash, Red Maple LifeSciences

Alec and William are the co-founders of Red Maple LifeSciences, a startup out of Brown University focused on developing wound care technologies for infection management and accelerated healing. Alec recently completed his PhD in Biomedical Engineering at Brown, where his research focused on wound infections, biomaterials, and smart therapeutic systems. William brings experience in medical device product development, commercialization strategy, and regulatory pathways in the medtech industry.

They were recently accepted into the National Science Foundation (NSF) I-Corps program, where they are conducting customer discovery interviews with clinicians, researchers, and industry leaders across wound care and infectious disease, with support from PAWSIC co-founder Julie Rhodovi.

"PAWSIC provides an incredible opportunity to connect with experts in the field, gain valuable insights into real-world clinical challenges, and build relationships that can help accelerate the translation of our technology into meaningful patient impact."

Past Member Spotlights

The clinicians and researchers building this community

Every month, our newsletter features a PAWSIC member shaping post-acute wound care through clinical practice, education, advocacy, or research.

Dr. Ron Rosen

Geriatrician & Hospice Physician

"What sets PAWSIC apart for me is the honesty in the conversations. We talk about what actually works in post-acute settings, not just what looks good in a journal. I have learned more from my PAWSIC colleagues in the past year than I have from most conferences combined, and every exchange makes me a better clinician for my patients."

Amy Rivera, RN

Nurse & Lymphedema Specialist · PAWSIC Board

Lymphatic patient, nurse, and advocate who turned her own healthcare journey into a career. Amy was featured in the inaugural episode of the PAWSIC Podcast, sharing how patient voice drives better wound care outcomes.

Isaac Zralii, LSWA, CNA

PAWSIC Board Member

Historic first CNA board member with over 20 years of clinical experience. Advocate for systemic healthcare equity, HIV/AIDS education, and LGBTQ+ healthcare access.
