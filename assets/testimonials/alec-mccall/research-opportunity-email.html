A Member Research Opportunity

Share your wound care expertise with NSF I-Corps researchers

Two of our members, Alec McCall, Ph.D. and William J. Ash III, MS, co-founders of Red Maple LifeSciences (a Brown University spinout), have been accepted into the National Science Foundation (NSF) I-Corps program. As part of this 7-week program, they are conducting customer discovery interviews with clinicians, researchers, and industry leaders across wound care and infectious disease.

They want to hear from PAWSIC members.

What you would be doing

A 30-minute conversation about your real-world experience with wound care: current workflows, common challenges, and unmet needs in infection management and chronic wound care.

No preparation required. No commitment beyond the call. Your perspective informs research focused on real clinical needs.
Book a 30-minute interview

The NSF I-Corps program runs for 7 weeks, so booking sooner is better.

About the researchers

Alec McCall, Ph.D. recently completed his PhD in Biomedical Engineering at Brown University, with research focused on wound infections, biomaterials, and smart therapeutic systems.

William J. Ash III, MS brings experience in medical device product development, commercialization strategy, and regulatory pathways within the medtech industry.

Connecting wound care professionals with researchers and innovators working on the future of our field is one of the things PAWSIC was built for. Thank you for being part of this community.
