From the PAWSIC Team

Two of our members, Alec McCall, Ph.D. and William J. Ash III, MS, co-founders of Red Maple LifeSciences (a Brown University spinout), have been accepted into the National Science Foundation (NSF) I-Corps program.

As part of this 7-week program, they are conducting customer discovery interviews with clinicians, researchers, and industry leaders across wound care and infectious disease. They want to hear from PAWSIC members.

"PAWSIC provides an incredible opportunity to connect with experts in the field and build relationships that help accelerate the translation of our technology into meaningful patient impact."

— Alec McCall, Ph.D., PAWSIC Member
Meet the Researchers

Alec McCall, Ph.D.

Chief Executive Officer, Red Maple LifeSciences

Recently completed his PhD in Biomedical Engineering at Brown University. Research focused on wound infections, biomaterials, and smart therapeutic systems.

William J. Ash III, MS

Chief Operating Officer, Red Maple LifeSciences

Brings experience in medical device product development, commercialization strategy, and regulatory pathways in the medtech industry.
What you would be doing

A 30-minute conversation about your real-world experience with wound care: current workflows, common challenges, and unmet needs in infection management and chronic wound care.

No preparation required. Just show up and share what you know from daily clinical practice.

No commitment beyond the call. This is a one-time conversation, not an ongoing role.

Your perspective directly informs research focused on real clinical needs, not theory.
