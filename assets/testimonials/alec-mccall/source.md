## Bio (as submitted)

Alec McCall, Ph.D. and William J. Ash III, MS are the co-founders of Red Maple LifeSciences, a startup out of Brown University focused on developing and commercializing next-generation wound care technologies for infection management and accelerated healing.

Alec recently completed his PhD in Biomedical Engineering at Brown University, where his research focused on wound infections, biomaterials, and smart therapeutic systems. William brings experience in medical device product development, commercialization strategy, and regulatory pathways within the medtech industry.

Together, they were recently accepted into the National Science Foundation (NSF) I-Corps program, a nationally recognized innovation and entrepreneurship program designed to help researchers and startups better understand real-world market and clinical needs. Through the 7-week program, Alec and William, along with the help of PAWSIC co-founder, Julie Rhodovi, are conducting extensive customer discovery interviews with clinicians, researchers, industry leaders, and stakeholders across the wound care and infectious disease space to better understand current challenges, workflows, and unmet needs within infection management and chronic wound care.

## Why PAWSIC? (as submitted)

We joined PAWSIC because of its strong network of innovators, researchers, clinicians, and industry leaders focused on advancing wound healing and infection care. As an early-stage company developing next-generation wound technologies, PAWSIC provides an incredible opportunity to connect with experts in the field, gain valuable insights into real-world clinical challenges, and build relationships that can help accelerate the translation of our technology into meaningful patient impact. Through the NSF I-Corps program, these conversations are especially valuable as we work to ensure our solutions align with the needs of clinicians, patients, and the broader wound care ecosystem.
